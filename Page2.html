Arthashastra SUBJECTS

Law and Government

The ancient Sanskrit text opens in Book 1 by acknowledging multiple schools of thought on governance and knowledge, including the schools of Brihaspati, Usanas, Manu, and Kautilya.

The root of happiness is Dharma, the root of Dharma is Artha, the root of Artha is right governance, the root of right governance is victorious inner-restraint. — Chanakya Sutra 1–6

Different schools proposed varying essential branches of knowledge. The Arthashastra ultimately proposes four necessary fields: the Vedas, Anvikshaki (reasoning), the science of government, and economics. From these four arise all knowledge, prosperity, and order.

Without government, disorder rises like the law of fishes — the strong swallow the weak. With governance, the weak resist the strong.
